# Cover Letter Framework

Most companies don't read cover letters. For the ones that do, keep it short (250–350 words), skim-friendly, and specific to the company. Generic flattery is worse than no letter.

This is a framework, not a template to paste verbatim. Fill in the bracketed sections per application.

---

## The 4-paragraph structure

### 1. Opening hook — who you are in one sentence

```
Dear [Hiring Manager / Team],

I'm writing to apply for the [Role Title] position at [Company]. I'm a
[your current title] with [years] years of experience in [your 2-3 core
strengths], and I'm drawn to this role because [the specific thing about
the JD or product that you actually care about].
```

The last clause is the only one that should change across applications. If you can't fill it in genuinely, this isn't a company where a cover letter will help you.

### 2. What you bring — 3 concrete bullets

```
What I bring:
- [Metric-driven accomplishment that maps to a top requirement in the JD]
- [Second accomplishment, ideally from a different context for breadth]
- [Third accomplishment, or a relevant project you shipped on your own]
```

Rules for good bullets:
- Start with a verb (*"Led..."*, *"Built..."*, *"Shipped..."*, *"Reduced..."*)
- Include a number (users served, latency cut, tests written, revenue impact, team size)
- Keep each bullet to one sentence

### 3. Why this company specifically

```
[1-2 sentences about the company. Reference their product, their tech
stack, a recent launch, a blog post, or a specific problem they're solving.
Connect your experience to their challenge — don't just compliment them.]
```

Before you write this paragraph, spend 5 minutes on:
- [ ] The company's product page (what do they actually do?)
- [ ] Their engineering blog (what have they shipped recently?)
- [ ] The JD itself (what specific problem is this role solving?)
- [ ] Any recent news (funding, launches, pivots)

If nothing comes back from that research, consider whether you actually want the job.

### 4. Close

```
I'd welcome the chance to talk about how my background in [top skill]
could contribute to [specific team/product]. My resume and portfolio
are attached (or linked below).

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[email] | [portfolio] | [github]
```

---

## Length guidance

- **Portal text box (small)**: 150–200 words. Drop paragraph 2 entirely; combine 1 and 3.
- **Portal text box (large) or attached file**: 250–350 words. Full 4-paragraph structure.
- **Never exceed 400 words.** If you have more to say, put it in a follow-up message after the initial screen.

The first paragraph does 80% of the work. If a hiring manager reads only the first two sentences (which is likely), those two sentences should be enough.
